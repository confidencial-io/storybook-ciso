Oh, The Data
You'll Inherit!

Bedtime reading for the newly appointed CISO,
the recently promoted, and the chronically accountable.

The badge is brand new,
the office is grand.
They said, "You're our CISO!"
and shook your good hand.

Oh, the meetings you'll have!
The budgets you'll pitch!
The frameworks to govern!
The policies to stitch!

But first, there's a thing
they forgot to explain…

Welcome aboard! (The real adventure starts… now.)
1
2
3

Beneath your new desk
sits a towering mass—
of tools upon tools
from leadership past.

A DLP from the days
of Obama's first term.
Three CASBs. A DSPM
nobody can confirm.

And forty-seven AI tools
(the count is ongoing)
ingesting your crown jewels
with nowhere near knowing.

4

"We know where it lives,"
said the dashboard with cheer.
But then someone clicked
"share externally here."

It slipped to a vendor.
It jumped in a bot.
It went to that RAG pipeline
nobody quite got.

It traveled to Slack.
It went home on a Mac.
And your dashboard just sat there.
It didn't look back.

The data left. The dashboard stayed happy.
5
6
7

The dashboard glowed green,
such a beautiful hue.
"No issues!" it cheered.
"No anomalies! Woo!"

But green only means
what the tools chose to see.
And none of them follow
where the data runs free.

Once the file left the system,
the green turned a lie.
The dashboard stayed happy.
The data said “bye”!

8

And then, one fine Tuesday,
a voice from the room:

"Can you tell us exactly
who accessed the files from Zoom?"

"Which AI tools ate
our contract last spring?
And what did those forty-seven
shadow AIs bring?"

You open your laptop.
You click on the thing.
The dashboard says green.
The board says nothing.

10
11

But here is the thing
that nobody told you:
the fix isn't another
dashboard to hold you.

Your DSPM still works.
Your DLP gets to stay.
Just add what was missing:
protection that travels the way.

Not stuck in the system.
Not tied to one tool.
It moves with the data.
Now that is the rule.

Protection that travels with the file. Every time.
12

The system's not the perimeter.
The file is. Full stop.

Let them marinate a bit.
They start making sense
on the Tuesday you sit
across from the board
who are staring at you—

and you have the answer.

The data stayed true.

The file is the perimeter.
Five words. Write them down.

14
15

So go forth, brave CISO,
with your badge and your chair.
You inherited problems
that were already there.

But now you know better—
the data's the thing.
Not the network, the system,
or the dashboards that ding.

The file is the perimeter.
Sleep well. Rest your head.
The protection travels with it.
(that's what Confidencial said)

🌙 The End.
Sweet dreams, CISO. Your data is guarded.
